Systematic Investment Plan (SIP)


SIP is known as a Systematic Investment Plan which gives the facility offered by Mutual Funds to the investors to invest in a disciplined manner. SIP facility allows an investor to invest a fixed amount of money at predefined intervals in the selected mutual fund scheme. A SIP is a mere tool that helps you to invest regularly in mutual fund schemes, mostly in equity mutual fund schemes. A SIP helps you to stagger your investments in equity mutual fund schemes over a period. Most mutual fund advisors do not recommend investing a lump sum in equity mutual funds.


A fixed amount of money is debited by the investors in bank accounts periodically and invested in a specified mutual fund. SIP works on the principle of regular investments. It is like your recurring deposit where you put in a small amount every month.


The following calculator helps you calculate the wealth gain and expected Returns for your monthly SIP investment. You get a rough estimate on the Maturity amount for any monthly SIP, based on a projected annual return rate.


Benefits of SIP

  • ⇒It is very flexible
  • ⇒No worries about market ups & downs
  • ⇒Plans are in budget
  • ⇒Convenience
  • ⇒No need to time the market
  • ⇒Power of Compounding
  • ⇒Rupee Cost Averaging

Advantages of SIP

  • ⇒SIPs are flexible and are an affordable investment option
  • ⇒Reduces overall Risk
  • ⇒Achieve your Goals
  • ⇒You become a disciplined investor and not a trader.
  • ⇒You need not worry about the daily NAV movement or index movement.

Disadvantages of SIP

  • ⇒You may miss out on the bulk purchase when the market crashes hence large return.
